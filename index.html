Master's Project: Confined Hydrogen Atom (Phase II)

▸
  • This second phase of the Master's project focused on the theoretical and computational investigation of the electronic properties of confined hydrogen atoms subjected to screened Coulomb potentials. The core objective was to analyze the interplay between quantum mechanical confinement and environmental screening effects.
  • **Key aspects included:**
    • **Electronic Structure Analysis:**
      • Conducted detailed calculations of the energy levels of the hydrogen atom under spatial confinement and in the presence of screened Coulomb potentials [e.g., Hulthén / Yukawa / Hulthén($2l$) / Exponential Cosine Screened Coulomb Potential].
      • Systematically investigated the combined influence of confinement and varying screening parameters on the electronic properties.
    • **Information-Theoretic Measures:**
      • Computed **Shannon entropy** to quantify the degree of uncertainty or randomness in the electron's spatial distribution.
      • Calculated **disequilibrium** to assess the deviation from a uniform probability distribution, providing insights into the system's degree of order.
      • By combining the Shannon entropy and disequilibrium values, the statistical complexity was calculated to quantify the structural organization and pattern formation within the confined hydrogen atom.
    • **Data Analysis and Interpretation:**
      • Extensive data analysis was conducted to extract meaningful insights from the computational results.
      • This involved visualizing data, performing statistical analysis, and interpreting the results in the context of quantum mechanical principles.
      • The impact of the screening parameters on the statistical complexity of the system was analyzed and interpreted.
  • **Skills Developed:**
    • Strong understanding of quantum mechanical principles and their application to atomic systems.
    • Experience in analyzing and interpreting complex data sets.
    • Expertise in calculating and interpreting information-theoretic measures, including Shannon entropy, disequilibrium, and statistical complexity.
    • Proficiency in data visualization and statistical analysis using Python, NumPy, and Pandas.
