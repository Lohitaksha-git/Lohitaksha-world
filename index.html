Master’s Project — Phase II

Plasma & Spatially Confined Hydrogen Atom • Dec 2024–Mar 2025 • 4 months • Guide: Prof. Kalidas Sen (UoH)

Focus: theoretical and computational investigation of confined hydrogen subjected to screened Coulomb potentials, quantifying how confinement and environmental screening shape the electronic structure and information content.

  1. Electronic structure analysis: energy levels under spatial confinement with screened potentials (Hulthén, Yukawa, Hulthén(2ℓ), Exponential‑Cosine).
  2. Information‑theoretic measures: computed Shannon entropy and disequilibrium; combined them to evaluate statistical complexity and characterize order vs. randomness.
  3. Data analysis & interpretation: visualization, parameter sweeps, and interpretation of screening‑parameter impacts on statistical complexity using Python, NumPy, and Pandas.

Skills developed: quantum‑mechanical modelling, numerical analysis, information theory for atomic systems, and data visualization pipelines.
